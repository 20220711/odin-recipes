What is Braised Beef

Braising is a cooking method that involves slowly cooking meat in a covered pot with some sort of liquid. In the case of this recipe, chuck roast is braised in a mixture of beef broth and red wine. Braising is similar to stewing, but it requires less liquid.

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ pounds boneless beef chuck roast
  • 5 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • 2 ½ teaspoons freshly ground black pepper, divided
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 4 teaspoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 pound cremini mushrooms, halved
  • 3 medium carrots, cut into 2 inch pieces
  • 1 head garlic, cut in half lengthwise
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 3 sprigs thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire
  • 2 cups beef broth, or more as needed
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Steps

  1. Season beef generously with 3 teaspoons kosher salt, 2 teaspoons black pepper, and garlic powder. Set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear beef until browned on all sides, about 10 minutes total. Remove beef and set aside.
  3. Melt butter in the same pot, then add chopped onion, halved mushrooms, carrots, and the cut head of garlic. Cook, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Pour in red wine to deglaze the pot, scraping up browned bits from the bottom. Let the wine reduce by half, about 3 minutes.
  5. Return the beef to the pot. Add thyme, bay leaves, Worcestershire, and beef broth. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Cover and transfer the pot to a preheated 325°F (165°C) oven. Cook until beef is tender, about 2 ½ to 3 hours.
  7. Check occasionally, adding more beef broth if needed to keep the beef partially submerged.
  8. Once done, remove the pot from the oven. Discard thyme sprigs and bay leaves.
  9. Slice beef and arrange on a serving platter. Spoon vegetables and sauce over the top. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley.
