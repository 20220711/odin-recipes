Bibimbap

What is Bibimbap

Bibimbap is a classic Korean dish that translates to "mixed rice." It is typically served as a bowl of warm white rice topped with saut√©ed and seasoned vegetables, a variety of protein options (like beef, chicken, or tofu), and a sunny-side-up egg. A generous amount of gochujang (Korean chili paste) is often added for a spicy kick.

The ingredients are stirred together just before eating, creating a delicious blend of textures and flavours. It is a popular and versatile dish that can be easily customised with your favourite ingredients, making it a satisfying and nutritious meal.

Ingredients

  • Cucumbers
  • Vegetables
  • Soy sauce
  • Olive oil
  • Seasonings
  • Steak
  • Eggs
  • Rice
  • Sesame oil
  • Sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Stir the cucumber pieces and gochujang together in a bowl.
  2. Cook the spinach until bright green and wilted.
  3. Drain the spinach, squeeze out as much moisture as possible, and set aside.
  4. Cook the carrots until soft, then stir in the cucumber mixture and red pepper flakes.
  5. Brown the beef in a different skillet and set aside.
  6. Fry the eggs until the yolks are runny, but whites are firm.
  7. Top each serving of rice with the spinach mixture, beef, and the cucumber mixture.
  8. Place an egg atop each serving, then drizzle with sesame oil, sesame seeds, and gochujang (if desired).
